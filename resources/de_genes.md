# Differentially Expressed Genes/TEs/Pathways

## Neurons:
**Stressed/Degenerating:**

* SINE elements (ALU) downregulated
* Downregulated genes: KIF5C-AS1, CCK, UQCR11, RGS16, KANK4
* Upregulated genes: TAC1, SERPINI1, SLC6A1, MTRNR2L12, RPH3A
* Pathways: modulation of chemical synaptic transmission, vesicle-mediated transport in synapse, proton transmembrane transport, neuron projection development, synaptic signaling, regulation of potassium ion transmembrane transport, neuron migration

**Dopaminergic:**

* SINE elements (ALU) downregulated
* Downregulated genes: KIF5C-AS1, PCSK1, GABRB2, GABRA1, PCDH17
* Upregulated genes: CD24, TAC1, AMIGO2, CDH6, WASHC3
* Pathways: trans-synaptic signaling, regulation of trans-synaptic signaling, L1CAM interactions, cell-cell adhesion, cell morphogenesis, synaptic vesicle fusion to presynaptic active zone membrane, intracellular protein transport, cell secretion

**Projection** (classic):

* LINE elements upregulated
* Downregulated genes: KIF5C-AS1, TMSB10, TPI1, SLC25A3, PCDH17
* Upregulated genes: FOXP2, FGF14, LAMA3, MARCHF11, PRR16
* Pathways: oxidative phosphorylation, modulation of chemical synaptic transmission proton transmembrane transport, neuron projection development, regulation of synapse organization, bacterial infection pathways, chaperone mediated autophagy

**GABAergic:**

* Only 10 DE genes
* Upregulated: SPARCL1, PCBP2
* Downregulated: KIF5C-AS1, ALDH1A1, SLC18A2, SLC32A1, GPI, PLD3, TUBA1A, SYT1

**Projection** (plastic):
* LINE, SINE elements upregulated
* Downregulated: KIF5C-AS1, NALCN, CDK13, PNN, CCPG1
* Upregulated: only TEs
* Pathways: regulation of membrane potential , heterophilic cell-cell adhesion via plasma membrane, non-integrin membrane-ECM interactions

**RGCs**: 
* SINE elements upregulated
* Downregulated: KIF5C-AS1, ALDH1A1, BEX1, SNCG, UBB
* Upregulated: PRR16, PWWP2A, ARID2, TRIM24, PRKCE
* Pathways: signaling by ERBB2, RHO GTPase signaling, trans-synaptic signaling, protein-protein interactions at synapses, regulation of PLK1 activity at G2/M transition, positive regulation of organelle organization
